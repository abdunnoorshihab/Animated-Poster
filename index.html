National Martyrs' Memorial of Bangladesh

The National Martyrs' Memorial (Jatiya Sriti Shoudho) is one of the most significant monuments in Bangladesh. It is located in Savar, about 35 kilometers northwest of Dhaka, the capital city. The memorial is dedicated to the martyrs who sacrificed their lives during the Bangladesh Liberation War of 1971, which led to the independence of Bangladesh from Pakistan.

Here are some key features of the National Martyrs' Memorial:

1. Design and Architecture: The memorial was designed by architect Syed Mainul Hossain and built with the help of the Government of Bangladesh. The main structure is composed of seven triangular-shaped towers symbolizing the seven stages of the liberation struggle.
2. Central Shaheed Minar: At the heart of the monument is the Central Shaheed Minar, a symbolic representation of the mother embracing her martyred sons. The design is intended to evoke a sense of grief, strength, and resilience.
3. Eternal Flame: At the base of the towers, there is an eternal flame known as the "Amar Ekushey Flame," representing the indomitable spirit of the martyrs.
4. Reflecting Pool: Surrounding the memorial is a large reflecting pool, enhancing the overall visual impact of the monument.
5. Annual Observance: Every year, on December 16th, Victory Day, the National Martyrs' Memorial becomes a focal point for the nation's celebrations. People from all walks of life gather at the memorial to pay tribute to the martyrs who played a crucial role in the country's independence.

The National Martyrs' Memorial stands as a symbol of the sacrifices made during the liberation struggle and serves as a place for reflection, remembrance, and national pride. It is an essential site for visitors interested in understanding Bangladesh's history and the events leading to its independence.
